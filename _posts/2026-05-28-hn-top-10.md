### 1. [YouTube to automatically label AI-generated videos](https://blog.youtube/news-and-events/improving-ai-labels-viewers-creators/)
⭐ 916 &nbsp; 💬 560 &nbsp; 👤 nopg
[🔗 Discuss on HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48299753)

YouTube is rolling out automatic AI detection and more visible AI content labels. Starting May 2026, if creators don't disclose AI use but YouTube's systems detect photorealistic AI-generated content, an automatic label will be applied. Labels now appear directly below the video player for long-form videos and as overlays for Shorts. Creators can dispute incorrect labels, though content from YouTube's own AI tools or with C2PA metadata remains permanently labeled. Labels don't affect recommendation or monetization.

### 10. [Rapira (Рапира) – Soviet programming language interpreter](https://github.com/begoon/rapira)
⭐ 19 &nbsp; 💬 9 &nbsp; 👤 begoon
[🔗 Discuss on HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48264705)

Rapira is a TypeScript/Bun interpreter for the Soviet educational programming language designed in the early 1980s by G. A. Zvenigorodsky as part of the Shkolnitsa school computing system for the Agat microcomputer. Faithful to the 1985 Agat dialect with Russian-only keywords, it features a lexer, parser, and tree-walking evaluator supporting tuples, sets, records, and the three-arrow procedure parameter scheme. Run it in-browser or via `npx rapira`.
